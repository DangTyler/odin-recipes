Lamb Shoulder Chop

Description

Lamb shoulder chops are a flavorful and versatile cut of meat taken from the shoulder region of the lamb, known for their rich marbling and tenderness. This cut typically includes both bone and meat, providing a robust flavor profile that makes it ideal for various cooking methods. Due to the higher fat content, lamb shoulder chops remain juicy and tender during cooking, whether grilled, braised, or roasted. They are often seasoned with herbs such as rosemary, thyme, and garlic, which complement the natural flavors of the lamb. Cooking methods that involve slow cooking or braising enhance their tenderness and allow the flavors to meld beautifully​.

In culinary traditions around the world, lamb shoulder chops are celebrated for their ability to absorb spices and marinades, making them a favorite for both home cooks and chefs. They can be served alongside a variety of sides, including roasted vegetables, couscous, or mashed potatoes, creating a hearty meal perfect for family gatherings or special occasions. Popular preparations include marinating the chops in yogurt or lemon juice, which tenderizes the meat while infusing it with flavor.

Ingredients

  • 4 lamb shoulder chops
  • 6 garlic cloves
  • 1/2 oz fresh rosemary
  • 2 tbsp oil
  • salt to taste
  • pepper to taste

Steps

  1. Prepare all ingredients for the lamb shoulder chops recipe.
  2. Chop garlic and break down the fresh rosemary; combine with oil, salt and pepper in a large ziplock bag.
  3. Add lamb shoulder chops into the seasoning and leave it to marinate for at least an hour.
  4. Preheat the skillet on high heat. Remove all seasoning from the lamb shoulder chops and add them to the skillet. Cook for about 6 minutes on each side. To verify the readiness of the chops – they should be cooked to 145°F for medium rare, 160°F for medium, or 170°F for well done
  5. Let lamb shoulder chops rest for about 5 minutes before serving.
